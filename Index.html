Conversation → Ticket Hybrid in Intercom

Our team is transitioning to a **Conversation-first with auto-conversion** model. This is a strategic shift from our current workflow, where live chats begin as conversations in LiveChat teams and are manually or automatically converted to tickets for complex issues.

Key Drivers for Change:

  • Enables faster resolution for simple conversations.
  • Provides clear accountability for complex issues.
  • Improves customer visibility with a formal Ticket ID and state.
  • Generates better data to inform our roadmap and operational improvements.

Decision tree

  • Start as Conversation
  • Convert when:
    • Multi‑team or >1 workday
    • Compliance, billing, engineering involved
    • Customer needs formal status
    • Bug or feature request

Workflow and automation

  • On assignment to Defined Team‑Inboxes → Convert to Ticket
  • Set Type and SLA, add watchers, notify Slack, send ack with Ticket ID
  • Ticket state triggers manage lifecycle and auto‑close

Ticket states (customer‑facing)

Submitted → In Progress → Waiting on Customer → Resolved → Closed

Roles and collaboration

  • Frontline handle Conversations, convert on criteria
  • Back‑office via private notes / Back‑office tickets
  • Lite seats enabled for cross‑team collaboration

Metrics (pilot, 2 weeks)

  • Directional read: CSAT and CCPH limited by window
  • Targets maintained for SLA; annotate dashboards as pilot data

Risks and mitigations

  • Over‑ticketing → QA weekly, clear convert criteria
  • Misrouting → tighten rules, verification queue
  • Change fatigue → playbook and office hours
  • Notification noise → consolidate updates

Rollout and next steps

  • Phase 1: Pilot complete, incorporate observations
  • Phase 2: Expand to SE, NO, NL team inboxes
  • Phase 3: 4–6 week measurement and optimization
  • Approvals: Owners, dates, reporting cadence
